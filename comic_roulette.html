Comic Roulette: Mobile App

Growing up, my friends and I would find ourselves in separate classes throughout the day at school. In the little time we had in between classes where we ran into each other, we found ourselves passing back and fourth partially completed comic strips. We would continually add on to each story, contributing one panel at a time, not knowing where the plot was going to go next.

To recreate this type of interaction in a mobile application, I created Comic Roulette. The goal of this project is to inspire collaborative comic creation. The app only allows one square of a comic to be drawn at a single time, and only provides the context of the previous panel.

The application has two modes of play, a local 'pass and play', and an online roulette style play. The pass and play mode is designed for social settings such as a party or a road trip. The online roulette mode is for a single individual to play. This online feature doesn't require an account or any other overhead besides an internet connection. When users play the roulette mode, they are given a random, partially completed comic. They can then contribute the next panel to the story, and the comic is stored away until another user is lucky enough to land on it.
