Comic Roulette: Mobile App

Growing up, my friends and I would find ourselves in separate classes throughout the day at school. In passing time between classes, we found ourselves passing back and fourth partially completed comic strips. We would continually add on to each story, contributing one panel at a time, not knowing where the plot was going to go next.

To recreate this type of interaction in a mobile application, I created Comic Roulette. The goal of this project was to inspire collaborative comic creation. When a comic is being created in the application, the context is limited to the previous panel, hiding away the rest of the comic's content. This constraint keeps each comic a winding adventure of zigzag-ing plots.

The application has two modes of play, a local pass and play, and an online roulette-style play. The pass and play mode is designed for social settings such as a party or a road trip. The online roulette mode is intended for an individual to collaborate with other comic-drawers around the world. This online feature doesn't require an account, only an internet connection. When users play the roulette mode, they are given a random, partially completed comic. They can then contribute the next panel to the story, and the comic is stored away until another user joins to add their creative thought.
