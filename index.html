Hi, I'm Adam!

I became fascinated with computers when I first attempted to play a silly Flash video game on Dial-up internet as a child. This sparked interest kept me close to computers and technology all throughout my youth. When it came to deciding what to focus on in college, I decided to pursue Computer Science at the University of Michigan.

During my undergraduate studies, and at a variety of internships, I found myself fascinated yet again; this time with developing interactions, tools, and programs that people enjoyed using. Seeing others use my creations and interact with them compelled me to study ways in which to optimize their experiences.

This ambition has landed me at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am currently pursuing my Masters of Human-Computer Interaction. Here I hope to learn how to make the right things: thought-out solutions to problems large and small, continually focused on granting users a wonderful experience.

I'm happy to say I no longer have to deal with Dial-up connections, and that my interest in computers continues to hold strong all thanks to that first Flash game encounter.
